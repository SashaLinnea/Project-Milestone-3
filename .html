About Me

My family is from South America, my mom and dad were both born in Guyana. Guyana is considered to be a part of the caribbean. I was born here in the United States (Georgia), but I still have the privelege of experiencing some of the culture (music, food, etc) from my family's home country. One funny experience that I had when I lived in Guyana for nine months as a kid, I would like to share. There was one night when I was sleeping, I randomly woke to find some sort of water bug/ roach crawling on the bed and vividly remember it biting me on the knee. The next morning I felt actual pain in my knee, but when I told my family the story they told me those bugs don't bite and that I was dreaming. Its something I'll always be confused about.

To go a bit more into my background, I moved around a decent amount when I was younger, so I experienced a few different elementary schools. I defintely think it made it difficult for me to make friends when I would be put in new environments that I had to get used to. I was raised by a single mother, and often lived in a large household with my grandma and a lot of my other family members. This is something I enjoyed because there was always someone to talk to and something to do, even if that was nothing, we did nothing together. I never had a close relationship with my dad, and its something that we're still working on till this day. I think the abscense of my father made me appreciate how close my moms side of the family is and hopefully always will be.

Seeing how hard my mom has worked and still does is a major motivator for me to continue pursuing higher education. Although I've never been passionate about work or a career, my desire to take care of my mom and be able to experience financial freedom is the source of my ambition. In terms of school, I have never been someone that learning comes easy too, but I've always maintained good grades by applying myself. The major obstacles I've struggled with have always been procrastination and a lack of discipline. I often wait for the motivation to come before I can do what needs to be done, but the motivation often isn't there. I am constantly working towards being a better version of myself and having that discipline because I know the consistency that comes with that will pay off in the long run.
